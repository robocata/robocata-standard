# Contributing to the RoboCata Standard

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the RoboCata Standard.

## How to Propose Changes

Changes to the RoboCata Standard may be proposed by any stakeholder. You can submit proposals via:

- **GitHub Issues** — Open an issue at https://github.com/robocata/robocata-standard/issues
- **Email** — Send proposals to standard@robocata.org

## Proposal Requirements

All proposals must include:

1. **Field impact** — Which fields are affected (new, modified, or removed)
2. **Backward compatibility assessment** — Whether the change is backward-compatible (V1.x) or breaking (V2.0)
3. **Rationale** — Why the change is needed
4. **Example implementation** — A concrete example showing the proposed change

## Review Process

Proposed changes are reviewed by the Editor and, where appropriate, incorporated into minor or major version updates.

- **Minor updates (V1.x)** maintain backward compatibility. They may introduce new optional fields but shall not alter or remove existing field semantics.
- **Major version updates (V2.0)** may introduce structural changes with documented migration guidance.

The maintainer retains final authority over acceptance into official versions. Future governance models (e.g., advisory board or community review panel) may be introduced as adoption grows.

## Versioning

- V1.x — backward-compatible updates
- V2.0 — structural or breaking changes
- Minor updates (typos, clarifications) do not change V1
- Each official version is date-stamped
